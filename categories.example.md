# Category reference sheet

This file is embedded verbatim into the system prompt sent to the AI. Use it to
explain distinctions between categories that aren't obvious from the name alone,
so the model categorizes the way *you* would. Edit freely — the categories
themselves are read live from your budget, so you only need notes here for the
ambiguous ones.

## Guidance for the model

- Prefer the most specific category that fits.
- When a merchant could belong to two categories, use the notes below to decide.

## Category distinctions (examples — replace with your own)

- **Groceries** vs **Dining Out**: Groceries is food bought to prepare at home
  (supermarkets, butchers, farmers markets). Dining Out is prepared food eaten
  out or delivered (restaurants, cafes, fast food, DoorDash/Uber Eats).
- **Household Goods** vs **Groceries**: Big-box stores (Target, Walmart, Costco)
  often mix both. Treat cleaning supplies, paper goods, and toiletries as
  Household Goods; food as Groceries. If genuinely unsure, prefer Groceries.
- **Transportation** vs **Travel**: Transportation is day-to-day local movement
  (gas, transit passes, rideshare around town, parking). Travel is trips away
  from home (flights, hotels, rental cars, out-of-town lodging).
- **Subscriptions** vs **Entertainment**: Recurring digital services (Netflix,
  Spotify, iCloud, gym membership) are Subscriptions. One-off fun (movie tickets,
  concerts, games) is Entertainment.
- **Utilities** vs **Bills**: Utilities are metered home services (electric, gas,
  water, internet, phone). Bills is for other fixed recurring obligations not
  covered by a more specific category.
- **Medical** covers doctors, dentists, pharmacy, copays, and health insurance
  paid out of pocket — not gym memberships (Subscriptions) or supplements bought
  at a grocery store (Groceries).
